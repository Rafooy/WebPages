Hideo Kojima

One of the Greatest Game Developer

Brief Info

Hideo Kojima is a Japanese video game designer, screenwriter, director, and game producer. In 2005, Hideo Kojima founded his studio called Kojima Productions, which was a subsidary of Konami. In 2015, Kojima Productions temporarily disbanded, but later reformed to become an independent studio. His notable works are Metal Gear Series, Snatcher, Policenauts, and Zone of the Enders series. He has been awared many times, but his most recent award is from Brazil Game Show which he was awardeed a Lifetime Achievement Award.

Early Life

Hideo Kojima is born on August 24, 1963 and is the youngest of the three in his family. He first lived in Tokyo, Japan, but moved around constantly. He always knew he wanted a career that dealt with creative expression such as being a writer or an illustrator. However, his family and peers wanted him to find a safer route. Despite being pressured not to pursure being a writer or an illustrator, he sent short stories to magazines, but sadly they never got published. During his college years, he studied economics and spent his free time playing video games. At the time video game was a new and developing sector. By the fourth year of his college career, he announced to his peers that he is going to pursure a video game development career. One his supports was from his mother who was a big influence on his decision. When Kojima reflected on his decision of joining the game industry, he thought it was the right choice because he said that the industry felt like a place where it can give them a second chance. Many of the people working in the industry were in similar situation and thought of games as second chance.

Loneliness

Hideo Kojima's journey to being a reknown game developer was a rough one. His early life was accompanied with loneliness. When he was 13, his dad died. Kojima was also a latchey kid, meaning he always had to return to an empty home. He often had to look after himself after coming home from school. The loneliness still affects him as he states in a 2004 interview,"whenever I travel and stay at a hotel I put the TV on as soon as I enter the room, just to deal with the feeling of loneliness".

Timeline

  • 1963 : On August 23, Hideo Kojima is born.
  • 1986 : Joined Konami's MSX home computer division.
  • 1987 : Kojima takes over Metal Gear project and releases it on MSX2 homecomputer for Japan and parts of Europe.
  • 1988 : Releases his next graphic adventure game project called Snatcher for MSX2 and NEC PC-8801 computer platforms.
  • 1990 : Kojima worked on two projects: SD Snatcher and Metal Gear: Solid Snake. Metal Gear: Solid Snake helped further evolve the stealth game genre. SD Snatcher introduced an innovative first-person turn based battle system which can be found in games like Bethesda Software's Fallout 3.
  • 1992 : Remade Snatcher for the PC Engine Super CD-ROM.
  • 1994 : Released Policenauts, a film noir sci-fi adventure game, for the NEC PC-9821.
  • 1997 - 1999 : Developed the Tokimeki Memorial Drama Series.
  • 1998 : The release of Metal Gear Solid help Kojima become an international celebrity in video game media.
  • 2001 : Release of Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty. Kojima also produced the game and anime for Zone of Enders.
  • 2003 : Produced the game Boktai: The Sun in Your Hand for the Gameboy Advance.
  • 2004 : Designed and released Metal Gear Solid 3: Snake Eater for PlayStation 2. Released the sequel of Boktai, Boktai 2: Solar Boy Django. Also released is Metal Gear Acid for PlayStation Portable.
  • 2006 : Metal Gear: Solid Snake released for North America and Europe which is included in Metal Gear Solid 3: Subsistence. Released Metal Gear Acid 2.
  • 2008 : Co-directed Metal Gear Solid 4: Guns of Patriots with Shuyo Murata due to death threats. Received a lifetime achievement award at the MTV Game Awards 2008.
  • 2009 : Kojima collaborated with Spanish developer MercurySteam to work on Castlevania: Lords of Shadow.
  • 2010 : Kojima and his team showed of Metal Gear Rising: Revengeance in E3 2010. Also remade Metal Gear Solid 3: for 3DS and renamed it Metal Gear Solid: Snake Eater 3D.
  • 2011 : Metal Gear Solid: Rising was renamed Metal Gear Rising: Revengeance and developed by PlatinumGames alongside Kojima Productions. Kojima is promoted into Executive Vice President and Corporate Officer in Konami Digital Entertainment. During E3 2011, he showcased a new innovative gaming technology labeled as "transfarring" which allows gamers to transfer game data from PlayStation 3 to PlayStation Portable. He announced that Project S will be a radio show sequel to Snatcher and will be titled Sdatcher.
  • 2012 : Kojima finished work on the Fox Engine and began working on Silent Hill game using the Fox Engine.
  • 2013 : At 2013 Games Developer Conference, Kojima revealed Metal Gear Solid V: The Phantom Pain which was his last Metal Gear series game.
  • 2014 : P.T. was released on PlayStation Store and it is revealed to be part of the Silent Hill franchise.
  • 2015 : Kojima parted ways with Konami. P.T. was removed from the PlayStation Store and the game was cancelled. Kojima Productions was re-established as an independent studio, partnered with Sony Computer Entertainment.
  • 2016 : Revealed the first trailer of Death Stranding. He also created his youtube channel where he talks about films with Kenji Yato.
  • 2017 : Became a daily contributor of Rolling Stone.

Fun Facts

  • The family had daily movie nights and Kojima was not allowed to go to bed until the movie was finished.
  • Kojima at first studied economics, but shifted his focus on making films. Eventually the focus became designing video games.
  • Kojima's inspiration for becoming a video game developer is from Shigeru Miyamoto's Super Mario Bros. and from Yuji Horii's The Portopia Serial Murder Case.
  • He draws literary inspiration from Kobo Abe, Ryu Murakami, Yukio Mishima, Kazuaki Takano, Project Itoh, Yasutaka Tsutui, Seicho Matsumoto, Richard Levinson, William Link, and Paul Auster.
